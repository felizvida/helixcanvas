# HelixCanvas Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

HelixCanvas is an open project for researchers, students, designers, developers, and educators. We want it to be a respectful place to ask questions, propose ideas, and collaborate across experience levels.

We expect participants to:

- be respectful and constructive
- assume good intent while staying honest
- welcome beginners and domain experts alike
- give feedback about ideas and code, not about people
- be careful with scientific, accessibility, and licensing concerns

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

- harassment or personal attacks
- discriminatory or hateful language
- intimidation, dogpiling, or repeated bad-faith engagement
- publishing private information without consent
- dismissive behavior toward newcomers asking basic questions

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to project discussions, issues, pull requests, reviews, and other community spaces connected to HelixCanvas.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this code, please contact the maintainers through the repository’s issue or contact channels. When possible, include links or screenshots so the situation can be reviewed fairly.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for setting tone, enforcing boundaries, and keeping the project welcoming. They may remove comments, close discussions, or restrict participation when necessary to protect the community.

## Enforcement Approach

We aim to enforce this code with clarity and proportionality. Sometimes that means a gentle correction; sometimes it means stronger moderation. The goal is a safe and durable open-source community.
